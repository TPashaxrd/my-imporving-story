Negative: dont/doesn't have to
Question: Do/Does plus subject plus have to?

I/You/We/They => Have to
He/She/It => has to

Zorunluk / Gereklilik
 - I have to wake up early. (Erken kalkmak zorundayim.)
 - He hast to follow the rules. (Kurallara uymak zorunda.)

Negatif Kullanımı
 - You don't have to come if you're busy. (Yoğunsan gelmene gerek yok.)
 - She doesn't have to pay for this. (Bunu ödemek zorunda değil.)

Kurallar / Yasaklar
- Students have to do their homework. (Öğrenciler ödevini yapmak zorundadır.)
- He has to follow the rules. (Kurallara uymak zorunda.)

Notes: 'Must' ve 'Have To' çok benzer, ikisi de "zorunluluk" demek.
Fark:
 - Must -> daha kişisel, kesin emir (I must study -> Kesinlikle çalışmalıyım.)
 - Have to -> daha çok dış zorunluluk, kural ya da gereklilik. (I Have to wear a helmet -> Kask takmak zorundayım.)

Mini Dialogue:
 - A: Do we have to buy tickets?
 - B: Yes, everyone has to.
 - A: Oh, I don't have to bring food, right?
 - B: No, you don't have to.
